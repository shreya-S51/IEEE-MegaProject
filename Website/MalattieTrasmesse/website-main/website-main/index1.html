About Our Project

DATASET


We used the dengue data set to train a SVM classifier which includes the following features, year, month, precipitation, humidity, temperature and number of cases per month. Since this a time series data, so to obtain better results we added a column of previous month's dengue cases. We used different models such as KNN, Random Forest and SVM. In the end, data trained on SVM gives the best testing result with mean absolute error of 64 and on malaria dataset we got mean absolute error of 17.

About Our Project

QGIS


We used QGIS to extract Senital 2 satellite images. We used intersection of Fraction of Absorbed Photosynthetically Active Radiation(FAPAR) to identify areas with vegetation and Normalized Deifference Water Index(NDWI) to identify areas with water. We applied thresholding in order to determine the stagnant water which are potential regions for dengue hotspots.

About Our Project

ROUTE


Usually there are many routes to visit from one place to another. This map will help people to visit from source to destination place by helping them select the safer route which is far from Dengue and Malaria hotspots. The map shows two routes, one which goes through the hotspots and the other which is much safer. This will help to choose the better path and hence avoid the dengue and malaria hotspots.
